Beef Stew Recipe

Ingredients

  • Beef: 1/2 kg of chuck roast, trimmed and cut into 1-inch cubes..
  • Vegetables: 1 medium onion (diced), 4 carrots (sliced), 3–4 stalks of celery (sliced), and 1.5 lbs of potatoes (cubed Yukon Gold or red potatoes hold their shape best).
  • Liquid Base: 4 cups low-sodium beef broth and 1 cup dry red wine (like Cabernet or Merlot) to add depth.
  • Aromatics and seasoning: 4 cloves garlic (minced), 2 tbsp tomato paste, 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce, 1 bay leaf, and fresh or dried thyme and rosemary.
  • Thickener: 2–3 tbsp all-purpose flour for dredging the meat

Cooking Step-by-step Instructions:

  1. Sear the beef:
    • Season beef cubes with salt and pepper, then toss them in flour to coat. Heat oil in a large Dutch oven or heavy pot over medium-high heat. Brown the beef in batches to avoid overcrowding, which ensures a good crust (about 3–5 minutes per side). Set the browned beef aside.
  2. Saute Aromatics:
    • In the same pot, add more oil if needed. Sauté the onions, carrots, and celery until softened (6–8 minutes). Stir in the garlic and tomato paste, cooking for another 1–2 minutes until fragrant.
  3. Deglaze:
    • Pour in the red wine and use a wooden spoon to scrape up the browned bits (the "fond") from the bottom of the pot—this is where the deep flavor lives.
  4. Simmer Sauce:
    • Return the beef to the pot. Add the beef broth, Worcestershire sauce, bay leaf, and herbs. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 1.5 to 2 hours until the beef is nearly tender.
  5. Add potatoes:.
    • Stir in the potatoes and continue to simmer, covered, for another 30–45 minutes until the potatoes and beef are fork-tender.
  6. Final Touch:
    • (Optional) Stir in a cup of frozen peas 10 minutes before serving. Discard the bay leaf and herb stems before ladling into bowls.
