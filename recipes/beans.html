African Beans Recipe

Ingredients

  • Beans: 450g Ghanaian black eyed peas, soaked overnight.
  • Aromatics: 1 medium diced onion, 1 tablespoon grated ginger, 1/2 tablespoon minced garlic.
  • Base: 1/4-1/2 cup palm oil, 2 diced tomatoes, 2 tablespoon tomato paste.
  • Spices: 1 tablespoon smoked paprika, 1 scotch bonnet pepper (optional for heat) salt and pepper to taste.
  • Flavour Boosters: 1/3 cup dried ground crayfish (optional for traditional smoky taste).
  • Liquid: 2-3 cups vegetable stock or water.

Cooking Instructions:

  1. Boil the beans
    • Drain soaked beans and simmer in fresh water for 50–60 minutes (or 8–12 minutes in a pressure cooker) until tender. Drain and set aside.
  2. Saute
    • Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Sauté the onions for 3–4 minutes until soft.
  3. Build the Base
    • Add tomatoes, tomato paste, ginger, garlic, and paprika. Cook for 60 seconds while stirring.
  4. Simmer Sauce.
    • Toss in the crayfish (if using) and simmer for 10–15 minutes, adding a splash of stock as needed to prevent burning.
  5. Combine.
    • Add the cooked beans and remaining stock. Simmer for another 10–15 minutes until the flavors meld and the sauce thickens.
  6. Serve.
    • Garnish with green onions and serve with fried ripe plantains or rice.
