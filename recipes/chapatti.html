Superb Chapatti Recipe

Ingredients

  • Flour: 3 cups all-purpose flour (or a mix of 1.5 cups white and 1.5 cups whole wheat/atta)
  • Water: Approx. 1 cup, lukewarm (add slowly)
  • Oil/Fat: 3-4 tbsp oil (plus more for cooking)
  • Salt: 1-1.5 tsp
  • Sugar: 1 tbsp (optional, helps with browning and flavor)
  • Optional: 1 egg or 3 tbsp natural yogurt (for added softness)

Procedure to make chapatti

  1. Prepare the dough
    • In a large bowl, mix flour, salt, and sugar.
    • Add 3 tbsp of oil/margarine and mix with your fingers until the mixture is crumbly.
    • Gradually add warm water, mixing continuously until a soft, elastic dough forms.
    • Knead the dough for at least 5–10 minutes until it is smooth and no longer sticky.
  2. Rest the dough (crucial for softness)
    • Cover the dough with a damp cloth or cling film and let it rest for at least 30 minutes to 1 hour.
  3. Shape and Layer (for layered results)
    • Dive the rested dough into 8-10 equal balls.
    • Roll one ball into a thin circle. Brush with a small amount of oil and sprinkle with a little flour.
    • Create Layers: Roll the circle into a cylinder(or fold into a fan), then twist the cylinder into a coil shape.
    • Cover the coils and let them rest for another 10 minutes.
    • Gently roll each coil again into a thin, flat disc, roughly 6-7 inches in diameter
  4. The Cooking Procedure
    • Heat a cast-iron skillet or tava over medium-high heat
    • Place a rolled out dough onto the hot dry pan. Coook for about 30-60 seconds until air bubbles appear and the underside has brown spots.
    • Flip and cook the other side, brushing with oil or ghee on both sides while cooking until golden brown and flaky.
    • Repeat with the remaining dough.
  5. Serving
    • Keep the finished chapattis wrapped in a clean kitchen towel to keep them soft and warm.
    • Best served with stews, beans or curry.
  6. Tips and Tricks for Success
    • Don't over cook: Cooking for too long or over low heat makes them dry.
    • Warm water: Using lukewarm water makes the dough more pliable.
    • Secret ingredient: A tablespoon of magarine in the dough significantly increases tenderness.
